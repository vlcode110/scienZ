Black body radiation

the ultraviolte catastrophe and the dawn of quantum mechanics

The absolute black body is the hypothetical body that absorbs the waves of all frequencies and emits waves of all frequencies. And these conservative guys run into infinity in their calculations. It was clearly impossible. There was a theory called Raleigh-Jeans theory. It worked fine with small frequencies, but as it reached the ultraviolet part of the spectrum, the intensity turned into infinity. This was called the ultraviolet catastrophe. No jokes, this is an official term. Because it marked the end, the collapse of classical physics.
At the end of the 19th century, the physicists thought, that they have ready discovered all there is. Nowadays sound impossible now, taken how many things have been discovered since them and how many things there are that we yet have no idea about, but at the end of the 19th century, the most well-respected physicists claimed, that the only thing they have left is to increase the precision of their numbers that are already known. The reason for this is that absolute most of the events that we see happening in ordinary life can be explained using two fairly understandable theories: Newtonian mechanics and Maxwell’s electromagnetism.
These theories work. Or, rather, worked. They start to break when it comes to a vey huge scale (like the cosmos)- this is the domain of relativity, and on a very small scale – the domain of quantum mechanics. In short, unless you have very big or very small numbers, everything is fine. Fairly, this was more of a self deceit, because they were still arguing about whether matter consisted of atoms or not, despite having the idea of atoms introduced to them back in 5th century BC by Democritus. Later John Dalton proved it.
There was a quote of Albert Michelson, a Noble prize winner:

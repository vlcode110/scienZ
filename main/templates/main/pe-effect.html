Photoelectric effect

Einstein's proof of the particulate nature of light

The photoelectric effect is a phenomenon that the 19th century physics was unable to explain. When you point light on an electrically charged plate, electrons start to get emitted. This contradicted the dominant theory of Maxwell’s electromagnetism, which stated that light is a wave. This was the proof that it is actually not that simple with light, one of the indicators of wave-particle duality.

Black body is a hypothetical physical body that absorbs the electromagnetic (light) waves of all frequencies, and, therefore, emits the waves of all frequencies. There was a problem with it called the ultraviolet catastrophe where classical physics broke down because for large frequencies (such as that of UV) the results yielded infinity, which is not possible. As any other body consists of atoms. Planck concluded that the the energy emitted by the black body depends on its frequency in a relationship E=hf, where h=6,63*10**-34 Is the Planck constant. Besides, the Energy can only be emitted or absorbed in quants, small discrete portions of energy, where one quant is the energy of one photon. This was the start of QM.

The experiment goes like that: cathode rays (which are essentially rays of electrons) are incident on metallic foil. When this experiment is set with electrons rays, it is unsurprising that electrons get kicked out of the foil. It can be explained by analogy with microscopic snooker balls. But when light does it, it should be a bit different. If it was a wave we were talking about (and light, according to the old-fashioned Maxwell’s electromagnetism, is a wave), than the electrons should gain the energy from the INTENSITY of light.

In his work on Brownian motion Einstein introduced the formula E= nhf It is just the hf from Planck multiplied by the number of particles. The hf component here means the energy of an individual quant, which proves that energy is discrete and consists of quants. In other words, a photon gives its energy to the electrons, but an electron loses part of it while getting to the surface. It happens because there is some work (P) that the electron needs to do to escape the metal, which would depend on the metal’s type. Photoelectric effect can be described by this formula: qU0 = hf-P qU0=E(max) E (max) = ½ m(e)v**2 qU0 is the energy needed to neutralize the kinetic energy and turn the stream of electrons into zero. , this comes from method by which the energy of electrons is measured. ‘q’ is the charge of electon and U is the voltage needed to hold it.
The important part is the second part, because this is what the experimentally right value equals to, thus proving that the predictions were correct. Initial energy of the electon plus the work done equal to the energy of one quant. All good.
Beside, this equation explained why there was a minimal frequency needed to kick out atoms. If F is not big enough to weight out P, than there is no point. And this solves the issue of the intensity. In Einstein’s model, the energy is transferred from a single photon to a single electron. But intensity is nothing but the quantity of photons. A more intensive ray of light will kick out more electrons, but would not give them more energy.

There is one experiment in physics which is used to demonstrate the wave nature of the light. It is called the Young double-slit experiment. Now it is also used to demonstrate the wave-particle duality. A ray is incident to a screen with two slits, that allows the light to fall on the second screen that shows a picture on interference, a pattern that is a property of waves. It appears because individual waves of light passing through slits disperse and get put one on another before reaching the second screen. Where two nods(bright parts) bright lines occur, and where two noughts (dimmer parts) intersect-dark regions.

Just for comparison, if we do the same with sand, it would simply form two heaps of sand. This is how we assume particles have to behave themselves.

And it is all good as long as it is light doing it, as we are kinda comfortable with light spreading in all directions and passing through both slits at the same time. But the fun part is: individual particles do it too. If you set individual electrons (which we are used to treating as particles, something we visualize as very small snooker balls), you will get the exact same particles over time. This implies that particles behave like waves: they pass through both slit at the same time. And this is one of the point where we enter the domain if weirdness of QM.

So, photoelectric effect implies that light is most clearly a particle, while the Young experiment implies that it is a wave. This looks wrong. But this is how it is. This is called wave-particle duality. This is the convention in physics nowadays. Photons (and particles on the whole) behaves like a particle in some cases and it behaves like a wave in the others. This arises many uncomfortable moments. For example, that a particle has a wavelength and a frequency. Or that a wave has mass. Somehow, mutually exclusive things turn out to be interchanging.

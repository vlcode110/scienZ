Standard model

the most successful scientific model in science

We have been told a million times that the atoms consist of a nucleus of protons and neutrons with electrons orbiting them. This is true, but a very simplified one. Today’s prime convention in particle physics is the standard model. The particles are generally divided into two categories: the leptons and the quarks. The leptons are those light particles like the electrons and its heavier alike types: tau and muon. They come alongside their neutrinos. Back in September, I made a talk about neutrinos. Now you can see where they are in the standard model and what they are actually doing there. Notice, that neither protons, nor neutrons are here. This is because they are not elementary particles. They consist of quarks. This is called quark confinement.

So, there are 6 flavors of quarks. They normally come in pairs. The up-down ones are those that matter consists of. The flavors of quarks vary in size and mass, with the up and down ones being the lightest, and the top and bottom ones being the heaviest. The strange one. I should note that the strange quark has strangeness of -1 and the strange antiquark - of +1.

Quarks never exist on their own. They only exist in groups of 2 or 3. This is because they have partial values of spin. I will explain spin in one of the following sessions, but for now I will just say that it is something that can only come in certain packages, as energy cannot come in non-quantized packages. See, all about discrete values. `Moving a quark away from its neighbors requires energy. This energy gets stored in the baryon or meson that is being divided. But, since energy is discrete, the more and more energy gets provided into the separation, the quarks will be produced!!!!!! They wouldn’t get separated, they will just multiply and produce more mesons or baryons in the ratio E=mc2

Antiparticles also belong to this group. Baryons include the familiar proton, consisting of 2 up quarks and 1 down quark, and its evil twin: the anti-proton, consisting of 2 up antiquarks and 1 down antiquark. They also include neutron, consisting of 2 down quarks and 1 up quark. Btw, antineutron exists as well. It has charge 0 and consists of 1 down antiquarks and one up antiquarks. The group also includes lambda. It is a zero charge particle which contains a strange quark. In all the rest, it is alike a neutron. But the fact that one of its down quark has been replaced with a strange quark makes it a strange particle with strangeness of -1. Of baryons, only proton and neutron are stable. The omega particle consists of three strange particles. It is very heavy for a subatomic particle and has a charge of -1.

With those particles which we do not hear too often about, it might be unclear what actually consists of them. Short answer: nothing of normal matter. These particles were discovered in the experiments on the large hadron collider. They appear in interactions of other particles and live for fractions of a second. Lambda, for example, lives for 10 to the power of -10 seconds. It might seem very short, but it was initially predicted to liver for 10 to the power of -23 second. It is its strangeness that caused it to live so long, and this is how this property was discovered in the first place.

However absurd the previous theory sounds, the origin of atomic theory actually does date back to ancient Greece. Specifically, to the fifth century BC natural philosopher Democritus. Alongside his teacher Leuccipus, he was the first one to suggest that matter consisted of smaller particles. They never perceived atoms as something too special, just understood that if you cut matter enough many times, eventually, you will reach a particle that can’t be cut anymore. “Atom” = uncuttable.

Mesons are the particles that consist of two quarks. More specifically, of one quark and one antiquark. Mesons are very unstable particles, and what it means is they have a very short lifespan. Some of the mesons are actually exchange particles, like the pion. The mesons are called like that because their mass is between the masses of a proton and an electron. “Mesa” in some of the Latin languages is translated as “middle”.
